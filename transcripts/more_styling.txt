## User

why doesn't vertical-align: top work?

---

## Assistant (Build · claude-opus-4-5 · 6.2s)

`vertical-align` only works in two contexts:

1. **Inline/inline-block elements** - aligns the element relative to its siblings on the same line
2. **Table cells** - aligns content within the cell

Buttons are `display: inline-block` by default, but `vertical-align` on a button controls how the *button itself* aligns relative to text next to it - not how the content *inside* the button aligns.

To control vertical alignment of content *inside* a button, you need either:
- `display: flex` + `align-items` (what I used)
- `display: grid` + `align-items`
- Hacky padding tricks

It's a common CSS gotcha - `vertical-align` sounds like it should work for inner content, but it doesn't.
